The Core Concept Secret Sauce

The Mistake: Most DIY "NFT" systems are actually accidental Deep Water Culture systems with low oxygen. People use round PVC pipes with high water levels, drowning the roots.

The Solution: True NFT relies on a thin film of nutrient solution (1-2mm deep) constantly flowing over the roots. The roots must sit on a flat surface.

  • Flat Bottoms: Use Vinyl Downspouts or flat-base channels instead of round PVC to ensure a thin, even film.
  • Oxygenation: 60%+ of the root mass should be in the air, not the water. This provides the explosive growth NFT is famous for.
  • Slope: Maintain a slope of at least 1:30 to 1:40 (approx 3%) to ensure flow and prevent pooling.

üõ†Ô∏è Step-by-Step Build

  1. Preparation:
    • Select Vinyl Downspouts (flat bottom) or 100mm PVC (if using square inserts).
    • Build a frame (wood or metal) ensuring a 1:30 or 1:40 drop (approx 3cm drop per 1m length).
  2. Channel Fabrication:
    • Cut channels to length.
    • Use a heat gun to mould end-caps on vinyl, or glue PVC end caps.
    • Drill holes for net cups (usually 2-inch/50mm hole saw) spaced 20cm apart.
  3. Plumbing:
    • Drill inlet holes at the high end.
    • Install a drain manifold at the low end returning to the reservoir.
    • Connect a pump (magnetic drive preferred) to the inlets using black poly pipe.
  4. Operation:
    • Fill reservoir (keep temp 20-25¬∞C).
    • Add nutrients (EC 1.2 - 2.0 depending on crop).
    • Adjust pH to 5.8 - 6.2.
    • Run pump 24/7. NFT roots dry out in minutes if the pump stops.

‚ö†Ô∏è Pro Tips & Warnings

CRITICAL FAILURE POINT: Never let the pump stop. NFT systems have no water buffer. If power fails, plants die in <1 hour.
Light Leaks: Ensure your reservoir and channels are 100% light-proof. Light + Nutrients = Algae, which steals oxygen and clogs the pump.
Pump Sizing: Aim for 1-2 Litres (0.25 - 0.5 Gal) per minute per channel. Don't undersize the pump head height.
